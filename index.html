  • Notes
    • While the article predominantly focuses on chitin, and does mention there are medical and pharmaceutical benefits of bioactive chitin oligosaccharides, I will only touch on this briefly (if at all), as the topic is enzymes, hence I will focus on the temperature and pH findings of the whole cell biocatalyst.
  • Background information that could be important to understanding relevance of the article
    • Whole cell biocatalysts could be important medically as it allows for enzymes sourced from different organisms (bacteria, fungi, other sources which are potentially dangerous, or targeted by immune system), to be added to a yeast cell, which can be used as a yeast vector for introduction of the enzyme to the host, which can have lesser negative impact on the host, which can improve effectiveness, reduce danger and increase efficiency.
    • Current enzymes are susceptible to temperature and pH fluctuations, having an optimum environment, points of denaturation and points of decreased and no activity. As an enzyme will have a very specific environmental requirements, this may not be suitable for all medical applications as not all environments are the same.
      • The use of recombinant surface display enzymes allows for enzymes to not only be introduced from potentially dangerous sources, but also allows for enzymes from organisms with different optimum environments to be introduced into an environment where other enzymes may not function.
    • Can be an alternative to chemical immobilisation (adding a solid matrix that increases the stability of the enzyme, allowing for it to be used in more environments) of an enzyme. While chemical immobilisation may sound like a good option, trying to add different substances to the enzyme could impact primary structure, bonding, structure and function.
      • These issues are mostly alleviated with recombinant surface display enzymes as they typically connect to cell wall proteins via the N or C terminus, meaning there is next to no modification of the structures of the primary chain(s) of the protein(s).
      • Chemically immobilised enzymes also can sometimes have limited or single use as a result. Recombinant surface display enzymes alleviate this as it is still essentially the original enzyme.
  • Article presented information
    • A whole cell biocatalyst is created through the introduction of different surface display proteins (which can function as an enzyme, or be an enzyme introduced from a different organism)
    • Chitin:
      • 2nd most abundant polysaccharide
      • Renewable resource with potential uses in biomed, agriculture, biotech
      • Strong hydrogen bonds and lower solubility in water or other organic solvents = limited potential applications
      • Contrastingly, chitin oligosaccharides (produced through degradation of chitin) are water soluble and easily absorbed by the body
        • Can display qualities such as: antioxidant, antibacterial, anti-inflamatory, fat metabolism regulation, blood sugar regulation, anti-tumor
        • Hence could have applications as anti-hypertensive drugs, anti-microbial agents and biological preservatives.
      • Chitin oligosaccharides are predominately prepared through enzymatic hydrolysis or chemical mehods.
        • Chemical methods are inefficient, required labour, time, cost and environment pollution through large amounts of acids.
        • Enzymatic hydrolysis through the use of chitinase is a specific hydrolase that degrades chitin by cutting glycosidic bonds.
    • Cell surface display technology
      • Anchor recombinant protein or peptide on surface of bacteria, yeast, insect or mammalian cells.
        • Hence, enzymes can theoretically be anchored on the surface of a cell
    • Yeast whole cell biocatalyst
      • Active enzymes are anchored on the cell surface by a yeast cell surface display system.
      • This has previously been used for medicine and food applications. Some examples include:
        • display amylase and Cellulolytic enzyme (to produce ethanol)
        • Display cyclodextrin glucanotransferase (to produce cyclodextrin)
        • Display lipase (to synthesise edible spices and perform hydrolysis of esters)
        • Display β\betaβ﻿-glucosidase (to convers isoflavone glycosides into isoflavone aglucones
    • Specifics and chemistry of the formation of whole cell biocatalysts through the use of recombinant vectors and specific cell surface binding sites
      • This is going to be way too much information for such a limited number of words.
      • basically polymerase chain reaction to clone nucleotide sequences, specific restriction endonucleases used to cut at specific recognition sites on a plasmid, such that the cloned nucleotide sequences can join into the plasmid. Hence creating a recombinant plasmid.
      • This recombinant plasmid is then induced into the yeast, where the proteins coded for (immunofluoresence, cell surface enzyme proteins, possibly antibiotic resistance) are synthesised.
      • This can result in incorrect plasmids, or some yeast not taking up any plasmids. Hence testing then occurs to ensure the yeast cells are enzymatically active.
        • Qualitative: Agar plate with chitin solution, if chitin is digested around the yeast cells, the correct recombinant plasmids have been taken in, and some of the yeast cells are now whole cell biocatalysts.
    • Effect of pH and temperature on the enzyme activity and stability
      • Note: DTT-released enzymes are partially purified (not whole cell biocatalysts).
      • Temperature
        • Both DTT-released and whole cell chitinase hole chitinolytic activity in wide range of temperatures (25°C - 80°C).
        • Optimum Temperature @52°C
        • Optimum range @44-66°C
      • pH (@52°C)
        • Some activity between 2.2-8.0
        • Optimum 5.0
      • Thermal stability
        • That is, activity after being treated at certain temperatures for extended periods of time.
        • The whole cell chitinase has a higher relative activity compared to DTT-released chitinase
    • Enzyme activity note:
      • While the stability of DTT-released chitinase is lower, its comparative activity is much higher.
    • Results implications
      • Thermal stability is critical for successful use of enzymes in many industries as it can allow for easier to meet reaction conditions (greater efficiency) when doing something like fabrication, or can allow for enzymes to be functional in environments that cannot be altered, such as a person.
      • The DTT-released (partially purified) enzymes have higher relative activity, but a narrower range of optimal environments.
      • The whole cell biocatalyst has a lower activity, but a larger range of optimum environments.
        • This means that there is a negative impact on rate using a whole cell biocatalyst, but this can be offset by the requirement for a larger optimal environment.
      • As the whole cell biocatalyst shows activity over a large range of temperatures, it is considered to be ‘thermostable’ (that is, immobilised)
        • As such, the DTT-released enzymes are less thermostable
      • While the whole cell biocatalyst may have a lower activity compared to DTT-released, due to the larger range of optimum temperatures, greater temperatures could ultimately result in greater for whole cell biocatalysts as reaction rate may be increased with minor impact on enzyme activity, where the DTT-released enzyme may have significantly less activity at that temperature.
      • The whole cell biocatalyst while thermostable, can also be resistant to other denaturing factors such as: organic solvents, detergents, lower and high pH and low or high temperatures
      • This thermostability and resistance to other denaturing factors is not shared with isolated enzymes. And is also present in chemically immobilised enzymes (however, these may not share all of the traits associated with whole cell biocatalysts)
        • Chemically immobilised enzymes also have additional issues: they can be inefficient, they can compromise protein structure of the enzyme and they can impact structure, hence be detrimental to the function of the enzyme.
      • One final thing to note, enzymes can be isolated from different organisms living in different environments (engineered naturally for different optimum environments), genetically engineered to work in extreme environments, or genetically engineered to work in a larger range of environments.
      • This ultimately means that the major issue of many enzymes often having a very specific required environment (in storage, and application), can be alleviated. Making them much more versatile for drug development, drug testing and clinical applications in the medical industry.
